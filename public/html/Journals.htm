Aim and Scope

  Food and Environmental Science (FES) is a peer-reviewed scientific journal that publishes original articles, reviews, research notes, and essays in the interdisciplinary fields of food science, nutriology, environmental science, pesticide and veterinary drug residue chemistry, pollutant control and risk assessment, toxicology, ecology, -omics and metabolic science, toxins, heavy metal science and so on together to solve key issues related to food and environmental research. Therefore, FES is committed to publishing scientific and technical information about the impact of natural phenomena and human factors on environmental quality, the relationship between environmental health and quality of life, and the relationship between social, cultural, political, and economic aspects, as well as legal considerations related to food tests and environmental medicine. As a comprehensive multidisciplinary journal, FES is composed of 18 main parts, including the following:

  • food safety and security;
  • food nutrition and health;
  • nutrients; functional foods;
  • metabolomics; multi-omics;
  • interaction between genes and the environment;
  • global environmental health;
  • ecotoxicology and ecological risk assessment and management;
  • environmental chemistry and computational models;
  • environmental education and public health;
  • environmental engineering and technology;
  • environmental epidemiology and disease control;
  • environmental genomics and proteomics;
  • environmental geology and health;
  • environmental health and disease;
  • environmental medicine;
  • environmental policy and management;
  • health risk assessment and management;
  • natural resource damage assessment and management.

  The journal emphasizes a holistic approach and will be a comprehensive multidisciplinary platform dedicated to solving important public health problems related to food safety and security environmental pollution and degradation.
